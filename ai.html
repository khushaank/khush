My Approach

Not “AI for the sake of it”, but concrete points in the research and investing process where automation and LLMs actually change the quality and speed of decisions.

01 · Idea Sourcing

From Noise to Shortlist

Turning thousands of headlines and filings into a small, high‑quality basket of ideas.

  • Use LLMs to cluster news and filings by themes (regulation, pricing power, disruption).
  • Flag companies with repeated mentions of risks or accounting changes.
  • Push the shortlist into Excel / Notion for deep fundamental work.
02 · Deep Dive

Augmenting Fundamental Research

Human judgment stays at the centre; AI cleans, summarizes and cross‑checks.

  • Parse 5–10 years of annual reports into structured tables and key quotes.
  • Compare management guidance vs. actual numbers using Python.
  • Use LLMs to draft an initial thesis, then refine it manually.
03 · Monitoring

Always‑On Risk Radar

Once a thesis is live, the hard part is staying updated without burning out.

  • Automate alerts for macro, regulatory and company‑specific events.
  • Summarise every new filing into “what changed vs last time”.
  • Maintain a living “change log” for each position in the portfolio.

Questions, Answered

A quick FAQ on how I think about AI in finance and what I can realistically help with.

Do you replace analysts with AI?

No. My goal is to remove the repetitive work (data cleaning, formatting, first‑pass summaries) so human analysts can spend their time on judgment, scenario analysis and risk thinking.

What kind of projects are a good fit?

Anything where there is recurring data to be processed: recurring management reports, watchlists, screening workflows, automated DCFs, or dashboards that need to be kept fresh with minimal manual work.

Can this work in small firms, not just large funds?

Yes. Even solo practitioners and small teams can benefit from simple automations: template‑driven client notes, standardised checklists, and semi‑automated research packs.

How do you handle data privacy and sensitive information?

Privacy is paramount. I prioritize local-first solutions where sensitive data stays on your infrastructure. When using LLMs, we use enterprise-grade APIs with zero-data retention policies to ensure your proprietary thesis never enters a training set.

Is this meant for high-frequency trading (HFT)?

No. My focus is on fundamental analysis and medium-to-long term strategies. I build tools that help you think better and research faster, not systems that compete on millisecond execution speeds.

Do I need a team of developers to maintain this?

Not necessarily. I build these systems using stable, well-documented libraries. Most workflows can be maintained by a single operationally-focused analyst, or I can provide ongoing support as your "fractional quant."

What asset classes do you specialize in?

My core expertise is in Global Equities and Fixed Income. However, the quantitative frameworks I build are asset-agnostic and can be adapted for Commodities, Digital Assets, or Private Equity benchmarking.

Can you help with portfolio risk management?

Yes. Beyond alpha generation, I design stress-testing architectures and custom VaR (Value at Risk) dashboards that help you understand how your portfolio might react to specific market shocks or regime changes.

Do you provide one-off consulting or recurring support?

I offer both. For some, a single "infrastructure sprint" to automate a specific pain point is enough. For others, I provide ongoing support to iterate on models and keep data pipelines running smoothly as a "fractional quant."
